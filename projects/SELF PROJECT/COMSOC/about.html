Our Story

Legacy of the Computer Society

For over three decades, the Computer Society has served as the academic and technical home of IT students at PUP Sto. Tomas Campus. What began as a small circle of computing enthusiasts meeting informally to trade notes on programming and hardware has grown into a full-fledged organization anchoring seminars, competitions, and outreach programs across the university.

In its earliest years, the organization was little more than a handful of students drawn together by a shared curiosity for computing at a time when access to technology on campus was scarce. What they lacked in resources, they made up for in initiative — organizing informal study sessions, borrowing equipment where they could, and pushing one another to learn faster than the curriculum alone allowed. That spirit of self-driven learning became the seed from which the Computer Society would eventually grow.

As enrollment in information technology programs expanded, so did the organization's reach. What started as a small circle formalized into a recognized student body with its own set of officers, committees, and a growing calendar of activities. Seminars and workshops became regular fixtures, giving members hands-on exposure to skills that classroom instruction alone couldn't always provide. Partnerships with industry practitioners and fellow institutions opened doors that had once seemed out of reach for a campus-based student organization.

Through the years, COMSOC weathered changes in leadership, shifts in technology, and the challenges of keeping an all-volunteer student organization running — including the disruptions brought on by the pandemic, which forced the group to reinvent how it engaged its members through online platforms and virtual programming. Each generation of officers left its own mark, expanding what the organization could offer while holding on to the values that defined it from the start.

Guided by generations of student leaders and faculty advisers, COMSOC continues to champion academic excellence, industry readiness, and a culture of service — values reflected in every initiative it undertakes, from technical bootcamps and hackathons to community-driven digital literacy drives in partner barangays. The organization has also grown beyond the purely academic, cultivating a competitive esports program under the Sentinel banner and a publication arm that documents the community's ongoing story.

Today, the organization stands as one of the campus's most active student bodies — home to its committees, its varsity esports program, and a growing roster of members preparing for careers in technology. Its history is still being written by the students who show up, term after term, to keep that original spirit of curiosity and service alive.
